# INSDR — Founder Narrative Questions

> For Vanness Wu. These questions are designed to extract the real story behind INSDR — not a polished brand statement, but the personal truth that makes someone believe. The answers will shape the founder narrative, launch content, and brand voice.

---

## The Origin

1. When did you first start paying attention to what you were putting in your body — not casually, but seriously? What triggered that shift?

2. Was there a specific moment — an injury, a health scare, a conversation, something you read — where you realized the supplement industry was broken?

3. When did the connection between what goes IN your body and what goes ON it click for you? Was that always linked, or did one lead to the other?

4. What were you taking before? What supplements did you trust, and when did you start questioning them?

5. Have you ever felt the difference between a supplement that works and one that doesn't? What did that feel like?

---

## The Problem — Personal

6. What's the worst supplement experience you've had? Something you trusted that turned out to be garbage.

7. When did you first learn about PFAs in activewear? What was your reaction?

8. What do you currently wear to train? Has that changed over the years? What bothers you about what's available?

9. What does your daily wellness routine look like right now — morning to night? Be specific.

10. What do your peers in the entertainment and fitness world take? Do you see the same blind spots in how they approach wellness?

---

## The Why

11. Why does this need to exist? Not the business case — the personal reason. What pisses you off enough to build this?

12. Who are you building this for? Not the demographic — the person. Describe them. Do you know someone who IS this customer?

13. If INSDR didn't exist, what would you wish someone would make?

14. What do you want people to feel when they take an INSDR supplement for the first time? When they put on the clothing for the first time?

15. What's the one thing about health and wellness that you wish everyone knew but almost nobody does?

---

## The Personal Edge

16. How has your relationship with your body changed as you've gotten older? What do you know at 40+ that you didn't know at 25?

17. What does "invincible" mean to you? Not the marketing line — personally. When have you felt it?

18. You've had a career where your body is part of the product — performing, training, being visible. How does that shape how you think about longevity and vitality?

19. What does your training look like now vs. ten years ago? What's changed and why?

20. Is there a health or wellness practice you do that most people would find surprising or wouldn't expect from you?

---

## The Culture

21. How do you think about style and clothing — not fashion, but personal style? What matters to you when you get dressed?

22. What brands do you respect — in any category — and why? What do they do that others don't?

23. You've lived across multiple cultures — Taiwan, the US, Asia broadly. How does wellness culture differ across those worlds? What does INSDR take from each?

24. The name INSDR — "insider." What does being an insider mean to you? When have you felt like one? When have you not?

25. INSDR is positioned as "if you know, you know." What's something in your life where you were early — where you knew before everyone else caught on?

---

## The Vision

26. In five years, what does INSDR look like if everything goes right?

27. What does INSDR never become? What's the line you won't cross?

28. If someone's wearing INSDR in the gym five years from now, what do people assume about that person?

29. What role does community play in this? What does the "insider" community actually look like?

30. You could put your name behind anything. Why wellness? Why supplements and cotton? Why this?

---

## The Unscripted

31. What's a health or wellness opinion you hold that's unpopular or goes against mainstream advice?

32. What's the most important thing you've learned about your body in the last five years?

33. Is there a moment in your career — a performance, a shoot, a tour — where your physical state directly affected the outcome? Where you felt the cost of NOT being dialed in?

34. Who put you on to something — a supplement, a practice, a piece of knowledge — that changed how you live?

35. What would you tell your 25-year-old self about health?

---

## For the Narrative Arc

*These final questions help structure the story: where he was → what changed → where he is → what he's building.*

36. Describe a "before" version of yourself — before you had this knowledge, before you paid attention. What did that version of you look like?

37. What was the turning point? The moment you went from consumer to "I need to build this myself"?

38. What have you tried or built before that didn't work or wasn't right? What did you learn from that?

39. What's at stake if INSDR doesn't succeed? Not financially — personally. Why does this one matter?

40. How do you want to be remembered — not as a performer, but as someone who built something?

---

*These questions are a starting point. The best follow-up questions will come from Vanness's actual answers — let him talk, then dig deeper into whatever lights up.*

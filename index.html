The Stack

Not trends. Not categories. What your body is actually missing. Every product has a primary ingredient, a synergistic amplifier, and a story that educates.

Product 01
Magnesium Complex
Body + brain + the key that unlocks both.
Primary
Glycinate

Body: sleep, stress, muscle recovery. Well-absorbed with significantly lower GI side effects than other forms.

Amplifier
Threonate (Magtein)

Brain: the most clinically studied magnesium for cognitive health. Patented, developed from MIT research.

Cofactor
Vitamin B6 (P5P)

Enhances magnesium cellular uptake. Mg + B6 shown to be superior to Mg alone in clinical trials.

The story: Most people take magnesium oxide — as low as 4% bioavailability. Two forms doing different jobs, plus the cofactor that makes both work harder.

Product 02
Mito Complex
Powers your existing mitochondria. Builds new ones.
Primary
CoQ10 (Ubiquinol)

Cellular energy production (ATP). Kaneka — the gold standard, backed by 85+ clinical studies. Levels decline after age 25.

Amplifier
PQQ

Triggers mitochondrial biogenesis — the creation of entirely new mitochondria. Also stimulates nerve growth factor (NGF).

The Result
More + Better

More engines (PQQ) × better fuel (CoQ10) = compounding cellular energy. This is the product customers feel.

The story: Your cells make energy in the mitochondria. CoQ10 powers them. PQQ builds new ones. Statin users — including a huge percentage of the older population — are CoQ10-depleted and most don't know it.

Product 03
D3+K2 Complex
Fat-soluble vitamin that comes with its own fat.
Primary

Up to 42% of US adults are vitamin D deficient. MK-7 activates proteins directing calcium into bones, not arteries. 72-hour half-life.

Supports bone mineral density and vitamin D utilization. Emerging research on healthy hormone levels. Almost nobody includes it.

The story: D3 is fat-soluble — absorption is enhanced ~30% with dietary fat. We built the fat into the formula. Take it whenever. It works.

The Case for Both
Supplements AND apparel — isn't that spreading too thin?

The combination is the moat.

Dropping either vertical makes INSDR forgettable. Here's why both are essential — and how they feed each other.

Apparel solves the supplement brand's biggest problem: visibility.

Supplement brands are invisible. The product lives in a cabinet. Nobody sees it. But someone wearing INSDR in the gym? That's a billboard that walks, trains, and starts conversations. Every shirt sold is a customer acquisition channel for supplements — at zero ongoing cost.

Supplements solve the apparel brand's biggest problem: retention.

Clothing purchases are infrequent — maybe 2–4 times a year. Supplement subscriptions are monthly recurring revenue. The apparel brings people in. The supplements keep them paying. One vertical drives acquisition, the other drives lifetime value.

The anti-synthetic narrative only works with both.

"Stop sweating in plastic" is powerful. But if you're only selling cotton tees, you're a cotton brand. Add "stop swallowing fillers your body can't absorb" — now you own a worldview. This isn't two audiences. It's one audience, served completely.

The combination is un-copyable.

There are hundreds of supplement brands. There are hundreds of cotton activewear brands. There is exactly zero that ties them together under a single philosophy. Drop one vertical and you become just another company. Together, they create a position no one else occupies.

The execution: sequence, don't split.

Launch the Foundation Stack as the revenue engine — subscriptions, recurring revenue, high margins. Alongside it, drop a tight apparel capsule — three to five pieces. Not a full clothing line. A statement. Enough cotton in the gym to make people ask "what's INSDR?" and find the supplements. As subscriptions build revenue, expand the apparel drops. Each drop creates a hype cycle that drives traffic to the supplement store. The two verticals feed each other — and that flywheel is something a single-vertical brand can never build.

The cross-section — INSDR's apparel DNA.

Principle 01

Material Is Identity

100% cotton isn't a constraint — it's the entire design language. Different weights, weaves, washes, and finishes become the palette. When everything is cotton, the texture tells the story.

Principle 02

Anti-Logo Confidence

Recognized through silhouette, material quality, and construction — not a chest logo. The INSDR wordmark lives on internal labels and subtle details. If you know, you know.

Principle 03

Construction Over Decoration

Invest in how it's built, not what's printed on it. Stitching, cut, hardware, seam placement. The anti-synthetic stance means the craft speaks louder than the graphic.

Principle 04

Assumed Intelligence

Don't explain why cotton matters on the garment — that's what the content does. The clothes just ARE. Speak to the informed consumer, not the mass market.

Principle 05

Curated Restraint

Launch tight. A small, intentional collection that's sold out beats a large catalog that sits. The Tee. The Crew. The Short. The Hood. That's it.

Principle 06

Typography as the Only Mark

No icons, no crests, no mascots. A bold, condensed wordmark that gains power through restraint. INSDR — or I-N-S-D-R — is the mark.
